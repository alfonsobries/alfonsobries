Traditional Catholic devotional painting, reverent and tender, faithful to classical iconography. Oil on wood panel with a subtle gold-leaf background in the manner of early Renaissance altarpieces (Fra Angelico tradition). Warm palette: luminous cream light, soft ochre and terracotta earth tones, deep warm umber shadows, garments in muted ultramarine blue and soft crimson, delicate gold halos with fine tooling. Soft diffuse candle-like light, serene gentle faces, graceful figures, calm balanced composition with generous negative space and a low quiet horizon. Fine painterly brushwork with the faint craquelure of an aged panel. No text, no letters, no borders, no frame. Square composition.

SCENE: The Visitation. Mary, young and expectant in blue mantle, tenderly embraces her elderly cousin Elizabeth, in ochre veil, at the stone doorway of Zechariah's house. Elizabeth bows slightly, placing a hand near Mary's. Behind them, the hill country of Judea with a winding path, cypresses and small stone houses under a warm golden sky.
