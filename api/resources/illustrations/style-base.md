# Behavior illustration style guide

This file is the shared style prompt for AI-generated behavior illustrations.
The `BehaviorIllustrator` service collapses it into a single paragraph and
prepends it to every request, together with the family member's character
prompt (`character-<member>.md`) and their style reference sheet
(`<member>-style-reference.png`) — a single guide image with the character's
palette, line style, expressions and props. Keep everything below written as
generation-ready prose.

Friendly hand-drawn illustration in the family's sketchbook style: loose,
slightly irregular black ink line art drawn by hand, flat solid fills, no
shadows, no gradients, no textures, no 3D, no realism, no text or letters.
Faces and skin are always the white of the paper, never colored. Each family
member keeps their own outfit and garment color from their reference sheet;
beyond that, use only a few soft colors per scene that harmonize together.
The tone is always gentle, didactic and kind — the scene must be instantly
recognizable to a 4-6 year old, and never aggressive, scary or shaming, even
for difficult behaviors. Composition: one clear subject centered on a plain
white or very light cream background, generous empty space around it, at most
one or two extremely simple props from the reference sheet, no scenery, no
visual clutter. Maximum simplicity: fewer details, more intention.
